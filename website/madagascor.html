Madagascar Almond Tree

  • Botanical Name: Terminalia mantaly
  • Common Names: Madagascar Almond, Umbrella Tree
  • Appearance: Layered foliage with reddish young leaves turning green as they mature
  • Fruit Production: This tree does not produce almonds or any fruit; it is purely ornamental
  • Height: Typically grows 10–20 feet in urban settings
  • Family: Belongs to the Combretaceae family
  • Origin: Native to Madagascar, widely planted in tropical and subtropical regions
  • Uses: Shade, landscaping, and aesthetic appeal in schools, parks, and gardens
  • Care: Thrives in full sunlight, prefers well-drained soil, responds well to pruning, low to moderate watering needs
